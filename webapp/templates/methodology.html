Methodology

Understanding how the model evaluates third base coach decisions.

Model Calculations

How the Model Works

As a runner rounds third base, the third base coach must decide in real time whether to send the runner home or hold him at third. This model reconstructs that decision using Statcast data and physics-based timing estimates to calculate the win probability-optimal choice, then evaluates whether the coach's actual decision aligned with that recommendation. The dataset includes 2025 at-bats with a runner on second base and a single was hit (excluding infield singles).

Step 1: Estimate Scoring Probability

The model estimates P(score): the probability the runner scores if sent home. A logistic regression model trained on historical send attempts combines 22 features from Statcast tracking data with physics-based timing calculations.

Step 3: Compare Win Probabilities

We calculate the expected win probability for each choice and compare them.

Step 4: Evaluate the Coach's Decision

Finally, we compare the model's recommendation to what the third base coach actually decided. Did they maximize win probability?

Missed Opportunity Held the runner Send (WP_send > WP_hold)
Suboptimal Send Sent the runner Hold (WP_hold > WP_send)

Limitations

  • Selection Bias: Coaches send runners selectively, so we only observe outcomes for plays they chose to send. The 97% success rate reflects pre-selection.
  • Estimated Inputs: Fielder catch locations and runner reaction times are estimated from trajectory data, not directly observed.
  • Unmodeled Factors: Catcher positioning, relay throws, defensive alignments, and runner anticipation are not captured.

Scoring Probability

What it is: The probability that the runner scores if sent home. This is the most important input to the send/hold decision.

How it's calculated: We use a physics-based approach that compares the time it takes for the runner to travel from second base to home plate against the time it takes for the defense to field the ball and throw it home.

  • Runner time: Distance from 2B to home (180 ft) ÷ runner sprint speed, plus any delay for line drives or fly balls
  • Defense time: Time to field the ball + transfer time + throw flight time to home
  • Scoring window: Defense time minus runner time. Positive means the runner beats the throw.

The scoring window is converted to a probability: large positive windows (runner beats throw easily) yield ~100% probability, while negative windows (throw beats runner) yield lower probabilities.

Scoring Probability Inputs

The following factors are used to calculate the scoring probability for each play.

Fielder Starting Position

What it is: Where the outfielder was standing when the ball was hit.

Why it's included: Determines how far the fielder must travel to reach the ball, directly affecting the time required to field and throw.

Source: Baseball Savant Fielder Positioning data.

  • Player-specific positions: Actual average starting depth and angle for each outfielder
  • League average fallback: LF ~297 ft, CF ~321 ft, RF ~296 ft

Time to Field

What it is: Time from bat contact until the fielder picks up the ball.

Why it's included: Establishes when the defensive sequence begins—the longer it takes to field, the more time the runner has to advance.

How it's calculated:

  • Ball trajectory: Based on launch angle—ground balls land quickly then roll, line drives travel fast horizontally, fly balls follow projectile motion physics.
  • Fielder movement: Starting from the outfielder's positioning depth, we calculate where they intercept the ball based on reaction time and sprint speed.

Throw Distance

What it is: The distance from where the fielder picks up the ball to home plate.

Why it's included: Longer throws take more time and are more difficult to execute accurately, giving the runner a better chance to score.

How it's calculated: Euclidean distance from the fielding location to home plate, derived from the fielder's intercept point.

Throw Time

What it is: Time from fielding the ball until the throw reaches home.

Why it's included: Combines the fielder's transfer mechanics with arm strength to determine how quickly the ball reaches the catcher.

Components:

  • Transfer time (catch to release): 1.1-1.4s depending on catch difficulty
  • Flight time: throw_distance ÷ arm_strength × arc factor

Runner Speed

What it is: The runner's sprint speed in feet per second.

Why it's included: Faster runners cover the 180 feet from second to home more quickly, increasing their probability of beating the throw.

Source: Statcast sprint speed leaderboards, measured as the runner's peak speed.

Runner Delay

What it is: How long the runner waits before committing to run.

Why it's included: Runners hesitate on line drives and fly balls to avoid being doubled off if the ball is caught—this delay reduces their effective travel time.

Estimated by batted ball type:

  • Ground balls: ~0.3s (go on contact)
  • Line drives: ~0.8s (hesitate to read trajectory)
  • Fly balls: ~1.5s or tag up

Scoring Window

What it is: Time margin between runner arrival and throw arrival at home.

Why it's included: This is the core comparison that determines whether the runner beats the throw—positive windows favor the runner, negative windows favor the defense.

Defense time = time_to_field + throw_time + catcher_tag (1.0s)
Runner time = 180 ft ÷ runner_speed + runner_delay
Scoring window = Defense time - Runner time

Win Probability & Decision Value

These metrics determine whether sending the runner is the optimal decision.

Win Probability Values

What it is: The team's probability of winning the game in each scenario.

Why it's included: Context matters—a run in the 9th inning of a tie game is worth more than a run in a blowout. Win probability captures this.

Source: Historical MLB data for each base-out-inning-score state.

  • WP if Send: P(score) × WP(run scores) + (1-P(score)) × WP(out at home)
  • WP if Hold: WP with runner on 3rd, batter on 1st

Net Runs

What it is: Total run value gained or lost from decisions.

Why it's included: Translates win probability differences into a tangible runs metric for comparing coach performance.

Formula: Net Runs = WP_delta × 10 (1 win ≈ 10 runs historically)
